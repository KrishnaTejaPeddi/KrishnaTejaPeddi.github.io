Today I learned that fireflies produce light extremely efficiently through bio-luminescence. they use a molecule 
called luciferin and an enzyme called luciferase, along with oxygen and ATP for energy. This chemical reaction occurs in a special
organ in there abdomen called a "light organ". What makes fireflies so impressive is that through bio-luminescence fireflies 
are able to turn chemical energy into light extremely efficiently, its estimated they turn energy into light arounda 95% efficiency, 
only wasting 5% on heat. modern day led's which are pretty good at turning energy into light work at a 40-60% efficienchy.
very impresive. I also find the way their light organ work to be really impressive,  inside the light organ cells contain luciferin and
luciferase, they react to make light. however for this reaction to happen oxygen needs to be present, and fireflies are able to control
the flow of oxygen in their light organ, so they can swithc on and off the glow when they need to. the way fireflies control the flow of 
oxygen is by constricting their trachae, which are basically tiny air tubes.

I also learned about mycellium. mycelliuum is basically the underground part of a fungus, the roots pretty much. its a thiny thread like 
structure that spreads through soil. it releases chemicals to break down dead orgaic matter and then absorbs the nutrients. it spreads 
grows these threads in all directions, when it detecs useful nutrients it grows more strongly in that pathway. It can also connect with 
plant roots in  a partnership called mutualism, where both sides benefit: the plant gives the fungus sugars made from sunlight, and in 
return the fungus helps the plant by delivering hard-to-get nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and water from the soil, sometimes even
from far  away areas the plant roots cannot reach. This network can connect many plants together, allowing resources and chemical 
signals to movethrough the soil, which is sometimes called the “wood wide web.”
